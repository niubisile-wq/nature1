# Terminology Ledger v1
Date: 2026-07-09

| Canonical form | Definition / usage |
|---|---|
| open InSAR observability bias | The structural bias introduced when public InSAR products are more or less observable in some regions or land-cover classes than others. |
| observability censoring | The mechanism by which poorly observable areas are excluded or underrepresented in public products. |
| monitoring debt | Areas where monitoring need is high but public product coverage or quality is low. |
| risk underestimation factor | A bounded comparison of exposed strong-motion area or population under visible-only versus full accounting. |
| area-weighted exposure | Exposure aggregated by equal-area or polygon overlap weighting, not point sampling. |
| hidden exposure | Exposure that falls in strongly deforming areas with not-majority observability. |
| lead case | The main regional demonstration used for the paper's strongest claim. |
| control case | A region used to test specificity or scope limits. |
| transfer case | A non-core product family used to test whether the same mechanism appears elsewhere. |
| no-token companion product | A public dataset that can extend the argument without requiring CLMS or EGMS credentials. |
| EGMS upgrade path | The future dense European benchmark route available once credentials are accessible. |
