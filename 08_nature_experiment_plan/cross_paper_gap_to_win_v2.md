# Cross-Paper Gap to Win v2
Date: 2026-07-13

## What Nature already has

- Strongest scientific breadth in the current five-paper comparison set.
- Transfer-aware, multi-region evidence instead of a single-case story.
- A frozen review-defense stack: claim map, risk audit, consistency audit, release-gap memo, scorecard, evidence matrix, and comparison appendix.

## What still keeps Nature from being the clean overall winner

1. Release mint gap
- The manuscript still carries `[DOI]` and `[REPOSITORY_URL]` placeholders.
- The local package is DOI-staged, not DOI-minted.
- This is the most visible reason Nature still trails the most polished final-note packages on submission maturity.

2. Hierarchical closure wording gap
- The all-region hierarchical artifact exists, but it is closure-by-compilation rather than a fresh all-region fit.
- That is defensible if stated explicitly, but it must not be narrated as a new validation split.

3. Comparison-pack gap
- The comparison exists, but it should sit next to the manuscript package as a first-class appendix.
- The reviewer should be able to see in one pass why Nature is the strongest science story even if it is not yet the cleanest release package.

## What closes the gap

1. Replace the DOI and repository placeholders with minted values.
2. Keep the all-region hierarchy described as a compiled closure, not a new fit.
3. Keep the cross-paper scorecard and evidence matrix alongside the release candidate so the "why Nature wins" argument is immediate.

## Victory condition

Nature wins the five-paper comparison when:

- the scientific story remains the broadest,
- the release package no longer lags the best final-note packages,
- and the all-region closure is described with the right level of caution.
