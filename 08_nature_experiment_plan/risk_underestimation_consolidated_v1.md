# Risk Underestimation Consolidated v1
Date: 2026-07-13

## Purpose

Consolidate the current visible-only exposure undercount proxies into one reviewer-facing table.

## Source basis

- `risk_underestimation_v1.csv`
- `multi_region_exposure_translation_v1.csv`
- `decision_facing_exposure_matrix_v1.csv`
- `transfer_validation_v1.md`

## What this table does

- It keeps the current underestimation proxy visible by region.
- It attaches the exposure-layer completeness status for population, built-up area, and infrastructure.
- It preserves the role distinction between lead case, regional benchmark, and control/specification case.
- It makes the transfer-status boundary explicit so the manuscript does not overclaim regional generalization.

## What this table does not do

- It does not replace the underlying Chao Phraya exposure closure.
- It does not convert the proxy into a causal damage-risk estimate.
- It does not claim EGMS closure.

## Interpretation

- Chao Phraya remains the strongest lead case because all three exposure layers are available.
- Po and Brantas remain useful regional benchmarks, but infrastructure translation is still incomplete.
- Indus and Rhone are better treated as proxy-based benchmark extensions, not lead-case replacements.
- Rhine remains the control/specification case and should stay out of any strong positive claim.
