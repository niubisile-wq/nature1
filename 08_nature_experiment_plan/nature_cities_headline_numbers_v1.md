# Nature Cities Headline Numbers v1
Date: 2026-07-13

## Purpose

This memo locks the manuscript-facing headline numbers for the Nature Cities version. It separates what can safely go into the abstract from what should stay in Methods, Extended Data, or sensitivity analysis.

## Locked headline numbers

### Lead-case exposure undercount

- Chao Phraya hidden population: `3,629,023.570`
- Chao Phraya hidden built-up area: `422.781 km2`
- Lead-case role: `urban exposure undercount` in the Bangkok-delta lead case

### Region-role support

- Po: supporting case
- Brantas: supporting case
- Indus: transfer/proxy case
- Rhone: transfer/proxy case
- Rhine: control/specification case

### Transport handling

- Transport infrastructure remains a companion sensitivity layer.
- The current transport summaries are not written into the abstract headline because the population/built-up closure and transport OSM anchoring are not yet harmonized into one single manuscript-grade total.
- Transport can remain in the Results or Extended Data as a separate infrastructure exposure layer, but it should not be used as the core headline number.

## Numbers that should not be mixed into the abstract

- `population not majority observable fraction = 0.170`
- `built-up not majority observable fraction = 0.326`
- `transport hidden fraction median = 0.531`
- `transport hidden fraction mean = 0.001`

Reason:

These values come from different transport / closure constructions and are not yet one unified urban headline statistic. Mixing them would create an avoidable reviewer objection.

## Abstract-safe phrasing

Use this style:

> In the Chao Phraya lead case, area-weighted exposure accounting shows substantial hidden population and built-up land inside strongly deforming urban areas.

Avoid this style in the abstract:

> The same effect also holds for transport infrastructure, with a precise hidden fraction of X.

Keep the transport statement in the paper only after the instrument definitions are explained.
