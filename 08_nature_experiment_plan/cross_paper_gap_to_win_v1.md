# Cross-Paper Gap to Win v1
Date: 2026-07-13

## Purpose

Turn the cross-paper comparison into a short list of concrete moves that would let the Nature package outrun the five peer packages on both science and submission maturity.

## Current advantage

- Nature already leads on scientific breadth, multi-region transfer logic, and claim discipline.
- The peer packages still do not match the same combination of transfer scope, no-token boundary control, and reviewer-defense mapping.

## Remaining gaps to close

1. Raw-input rerun gap
- The raster-backed Chao Phraya steps still reuse frozen outputs instead of rebuilding directly from windowed raw inputs in the current environment.
- Closing this gap would make the Nature package more reproducible than the benchmark-style peer packages.

2. Release gap
- The public DOI and versioned repository URL are still placeholders.
- Closing this gap would bring the Nature package to the same submission maturity tier as the strongest final-note peer packages.

3. Hierarchical closure gap
- The all-region cell-level hierarchical fit is still absent.
- Either a closed fit or a formal no-fit note is needed to make the cross-region evidence chain harder to challenge than the peer packages.

4. Comparison-pack gap
- The Nature package still needs a self-contained cross-paper comparison appendix that tells the reviewer why this package is the strongest overall one, not just the strongest science story.

## Execution order

1. Finish the raw-input rerun path for the raster-backed Chao Phraya steps.
2. Mint or confirm the DOI and repository URL.
3. Close or formally bound the all-region cell-level hierarchical fit.
4. Publish the comparison appendix and keep the other peer packages unchanged.

## Victory condition

Nature beats the five when it has:

- stronger scientific breadth than the benchmark packages,
- cleaner release readiness than the final-note packages,
- and a reviewer-facing comparison appendix that makes the advantage legible in one pass.
