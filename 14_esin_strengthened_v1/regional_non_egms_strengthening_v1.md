# Regional non-EGMS strengthening v1

Date: 2026-07-19

This file records the local regional evidence that can strengthen the ESIN manuscript before credentialed EGMS/CLMS products are available. It deliberately separates local observability/exposure replication from independent EGMS validation.

## Main additions

- Po is the strongest local European strengthening case already available in the package. The current observability synthesis reports 2,255 covered cells, 2,013 strong cells, an odds ratio of 2.6034 (95% CI 1.9528-3.4707), 0.6197 strong-population fraction, 0.3403 strong-population not-majority-observable fraction, 0.7080 strong-built-up fraction and 0.4603 strong-built-up not-majority-observable fraction.
- Chao Phraya remains the lead closure case because it has the most complete population, built-up and transport exposure stack.
- Brantas provides an additional positive supporting case with population and built-up exposure translation, but no completed infrastructure translation.
- Rhone remains a conditional upgrade case: the hierarchical ladder gives a positive regional signal (OR 1.5918), but current local evidence is partial-proxy only.
- Rhine remains a control/specification case with OR 0.8101 and should not be promoted as a positive replication.

## Boundary statement

These results strengthen regional transfer and benchmark prioritization, but they do not complete the A-priority EGMS validation gate. The decisive Po delta / Po-Venice / Rhone-Camargue closure still requires credentialed CLMS product access or manually downloaded EGMS L3 ORTHO-UP point files.
